# Session 16 Plan — Into the Storm

**Picking up from:** The party agreed to sail with Captain Hex to hunt the Shadow; Anders is imprisoned, Saltmarsh is in Eda's hands, and Winston has asked the party to help break his necromancer's curse.
**Session goal:** Establish the Shadow as a mythic, dangerous quarry; introduce the Lolth island as the first obstacle between the party and the ship; leave the players hungry to push further into the storm.

---

## The Shadow — What It Is

The Shadow is a phantom galleon. No living crew. No captain at the helm. It sails the Azure Sea surrounded by a permanent tempest — a storm system that doesn't move with the weather, it moves with the ship. Within that storm, islands appear and disappear: coral formations, fog-wrapped rocks, sandbars that weren't there yesterday. Sailors have been chasing it for generations. Most turn back. Some don't return.

**The curse:** Anyone who boards the Shadow and tries to take its helm triggers the ship's curse. The rigging animates and kills the crew. The would-be captain is blinded and bound to the ship — made its captain against their will, unable to leave or die. There is rumored to be one such cursed captain aboard right now, trapped at the helm, sightless, steering by feel alone.

**Krell's island:** One of those "moving" islands within the storm system is not actually moving — it's permanent, but the storms hide it from outside charts. This is where Krell came from. A cell of Lolth-worshipping island druids has made their home there, treating the Shadow's storm as sacred ground — Lolth's veil over the sea. They did not create the Shadow and do not control it. They nest in its wake, scavenge what it leaves behind, and kill anyone who passes through their waters uninvited. The Empress of the Waves encountered them years ago (the captain's logs the party read in Session 08 documented the destruction); Krell was one of the survivors who eventually ended up aboard the Empress.

**Krell and the Shadow are distinct:** The Shadow predates the Lolth cult on the island by centuries. The druids are an obstacle on the approach, not the destination. Hex knows they're there. He's been trying to find a way through them for years.

## Story Beats

### Beat 1 — Open Water
The party is aboard Hex's ship, a day or two out from Saltmarsh. This is a good moment for roleplay: Hex sharing what he knows about the Shadow, the crew's attitude toward the hunt, Winston's quiet worry about his curse, and what the party is walking into. Hex is obsessed — he's been chasing this for years and is finally close. He's not reckless, but he's not turning back either.

**What Hex knows (share what fits):**
- The Shadow appears on no chart, but navigators who've seen it describe the same star pattern in the night sky when it was near — a cluster that doesn't match any constellation.
- Every captain who's tried to take the helm has vanished. One returned: blind, raving, eventually dying from wounds that looked like rope burns.
- The island within the storm is real. Hex has approached it twice and been driven back both times by the druids.
- He believes the Shadow can be *claimed* — not by force, but by breaking the curse on its current captain.

**Winston thread:** He's sailing with them. His wing is still visibly corrupted. He's quiet but present. If the party asks what he knows about curses on ships or the Shadow, he doesn't have answers — but a DC 14 History or Arcana check might surface that binding curses to ships or objects is a necromantic technique, suggesting the same school of magic that cursed him.

### Beat 2 — The Storm on the Horizon
The permanent storm becomes visible: a dark wall of cloud and lightning that doesn't track with the weather. It's unmistakable. The sea changes — the color, the smell, the temperature. Hex's crew gets quiet. This is the moment the Shadow becomes real to the party rather than a legend.

A sailor might share a fragment of local myth: "The storms don't kill you. They just make sure you don't leave."

The storm ring is wide — it would take most of a day to sail around trying to find an entry point, and there may not be one. The only way in is through.

### Beat 3 — The Island in the Storm
As the ship enters the outer edge of the storm, the island becomes visible through the rain: dark, forested, with lights on the shore that move like carried torches. Hex knows what this is. His jaw tightens.

The island is inhabited. The party can:
- Try to approach carefully (Stealth/Deception)
- Attempt a parley with whoever comes to meet them (they will come — they always do)
- Press through the storm and try to avoid the island entirely (Navigation, high difficulty, risk of being forced aground)
- Fight their way through if it comes to it

The druids on the island are Lolth-worshippers — hostile by default, but not mindless. They consider the storm their sacred territory. They have spiders, phase spider companions (like Krell did), and knowledge of the Shadow's movements. They may be willing to talk if the party can offer something they want, demonstrate strength, or invoke Lolth's name in a way that buys them pause.

**Potential parley hook:** The druids want something from the Shadow too — they believe it carries an artifact sacred to Lolth. They might offer passage in exchange for the party retrieving it. This gives the druids a reason not to kill everyone on sight and creates a competing claim on the Shadow's hold.

### Beat 4 — The Shadow at the Edge of Sight
Whether the party fights, parleys, or sneaks through, close the session with the Shadow itself becoming visible: a massive black-hulled galleon, moving under full sail with no wind to drive it, silent except for the creak of rigging. No lights. No crew on deck. Just the ship, sailing alone.

It's huge. Bigger than it has any right to be.

The party is close enough to see it. Not close enough to board. End there.
